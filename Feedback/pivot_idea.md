Idea 1:
As this is a novel idea for a video editor, I want to suggest something else. I think we should have as much as possible that resembles a video editor, but the timeline automation might be better done vertically rather than horizontally. The idea I have is that when you follow a line on a circle that is rotating (similar to the position of the playhead over time) you can record the playhead along an oscilating curve. Here's another way to conceptualize this: "Arm the track, record enabled. Playhead becomes your live slider—no knobs, just time position. Move it however you want (linear sweep, circle‑mimic oscillation, freehand chaos), and that motion gets baked as raw automation data. Oscillator? Only if you make it oscillate like a clock hand around an imaginary circle." So basically, how a midi track would record a slider of volume let's say. 

What I'm thinking is that it would be confusing to have a timeline horizontally, but this acts as a sort of "automation lane" for the playhead, so I'm thinking of a timeline vertically on the right or left if that makes sense. Or even below but this might not work for UI.
